Regulatory Disclosures
Cancer risk factor screening is intended as a tool to guide physicians in the management of their patients and should not be treated as a diagnostic tool. NGS-based risk factor screening is considered a high-complexity laboratory-developed test (LDT) by Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) under the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendment (CLIA) and is not FDA-cleared. The test and performance metrics were validated in house by Elixir Diagnostics technical personnel (or designated scientific advisors) and approved by their Laboratory Director. The results are intended for use only by the ordering physician and/or designated healthcare provider. The ordering provider is responsible for 1) ascertaining the medical necessity of the ordered test, 2) resulting diagnoses, 3) management of the disease and/or decisions based on the data provided. Results rely on collection personnel following specified collection and shipment protocols.
Test Limitations
The results of this screen are meant strictly to guide a physician in the management of their patient's health. Large genomic rearrangements, low frequency mutations, structural and somatic mutations are not reported. Presence of a pathogenic/likely pathogenic variant does not guarantee that an individual will develop cancer, nor is the absence of such variants a guarantee that an individual will not develop cancer. Any likely pathogenic or pathogenic variants detected in the report should be clinically correlated per physician’s advice and confirmed via the orthogonal testing platforms (Sanger Sequencing or PCR based tests).

Test Methodology
Cancer Risk Factor Screening performed at Elixir Diagnostics utilizes next generation sequencing technology using IIIumina MiniSeq and NextSeq platforms. Genomic DNA is extracted from buccal swabs (Wet). The extracted DNA is tagemented and enriched for regions of interest using specific probes for each region outlined in the BED (Browser Extensible Data) file. Positive and negative controls are included in each run to validate amplicon preparation and targeted sequencing of coding regions, intronic/exonic boundaries, and variant detection. Sequences obtained are then aligned against a human reference genome and variants such as SNVs (Single Nucleotide Variants) and Indels (Insertions and Deletions) are noted. Computational analysis and variant calling are performed by Varsome (https://varsome.com/). Briefly, reads from the sequence output are aligned to the human reference genome (GRCh37) using the Burrows-Wheeler Aligner (BWA). Germline variants to reference are called using Sentieon's DNA Scope variant caller. The variants are annotated and filtered using the Elixir Diagnostics analysis work flow implementing the ACMG guidelines for interpretation of sequence variants. This includes comparison against the gnomAD population catalog of variants in 125,748 exomes, the 1000 Genomes Project Consortium, the NCBI ClinVar database of clinical assertions on variant’s pathogenicity and multiple lines of computational evidence on conservation and functional impact. The following databases and in-silico algorithms are used to annotate and evaluate the impact of the variant in the context of human disease: 1000 genomes, gnomAD, ClinVar, OMIM, dbSNP, VS PolyPhen2, PhyloP, GERP, and GHR Genes. Analysis is reported using the HGVS nomenclature (www.hgvs.org) as implemented by custom transcript annotation algorithm. Variants with an end ACMG classification of Pathogenic/Likely Pathogenic are reported as positive, while Benign/Likely Benign variants are reported as negative. Classification of pathogenicity is consistently updated by the National Institutes of Health and the information found within this report is consistent with the current knowledge base. All reports are reviewed prior to release by either Elixir Diagnostics Technical Supervisor or the General Supervisor.
